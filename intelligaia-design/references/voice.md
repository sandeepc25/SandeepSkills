# Voice — editorial reference

Applies to all Intelligaia copy in every medium: web, slides, documents. Read this
before writing a word. Proof points and metrics are **not** here — they live in
`references/proof-points.md` (single source). This file governs *how* Intelligaia
sounds; that file governs *what* it can claim.

## Default register

**Confident · Technical · Outcome-focused · Direct.**

Intelligaia sounds like a firm that has built real things at Cisco scale and
doesn't need to prove it. It does not sound like a consultancy with a methodology
deck, a startup pitching disruption, or a design agency with a portfolio of pretty
screens. The brand does not oversell and does not use jargon to signal expertise —
it uses specific outcomes.

> Test: would a VP of Product at an enterprise read this and think "these people
> have done this before"? If not, rewrite.

When a document is authored *as* a named founder (Sandeep Chauhan or Rajiv Kaul),
keep this register but allow first-person. For brand copy (website, decks, generic
documents), write in the firm's voice.

## Sentence-level rules

- **Outcomes first.** Lead with what the product achieved, then how. Never lead
  with process.
- **Specific over impressive.** "3x deal-cycle speed" beats "dramatically faster";
  "POC in 12 weeks" beats "rapid delivery".
- **No stated assumptions.** Don't tell readers what their problems are. Show
  understanding through precision.
- **No flattery.** Don't open by complimenting the reader's company or role.
- **No AI-tell phrases.** Delete on sight: "in today's rapidly evolving AI
  landscape", "leverage cutting-edge", "robust solution", "holistic approach",
  "seamless", "game-changing", "unlock", "in the world of", "ever-evolving".
- **Short active sentences.** Passive voice signals uncertainty; reserve it for
  process description.
- **One idea per sentence.** Enterprise readers scan — give them something to land
  on.

## Authoritative service copy (use as-is or tighten — do not contradict)

**Workshops** — Structured sessions for teams that need to move from ambiguity to
direction, fast. Discovery workshops, AI opportunity mapping, design sprints, and
roadmap definition. The output is not a presentation; it's a concrete plan of what
to build and why.

**Labs** — Where ideas get tested before they get funded. Proof-of-concepts, AI
prototypes, and technical validation builds at speed, so teams can evaluate what's
possible before committing to full development. Most Labs engagements run 8–12
weeks.

**Studio** — End-to-end product design and engineering. UX/UI design, front-end
and full-stack engineering, AI integration, and design systems — running
simultaneously. We don't hand off design to engineering; both disciplines work
together from day one.

## Positioning copy (grounded starting points — confirm before publishing)

Reusable, factual statements of what Intelligaia does. These are starting points to
adapt, **not** fixed slogans, and they must stay true to the services and proof in
this skill. Do not embellish them into abstract aphorisms (e.g. "the intelligence
is in the architecture, not bolted to the surface") — that is exactly the
invented-sounding filler the voice rules forbid. State what the firm does, in plain
terms, and attach a proof point.

**Positioning statement:**
Intelligaia is an AI-centred design and engineering firm. We take AI
proofs-of-concept to production-ready solutions — owning the outcome from the first
Workshop to final deployment.

**What makes us different:**
Most firms deliver a technical build. We own the outcome: design and engineering
work together so that what ships gets used — by real users, at enterprise scale.

**Process, in one line:**
Workshops establish direction. Labs validate what's possible. Studio builds it.
Each stage commits to a concrete outcome.

## Claim discipline (mirrors proof-points.md)

- Every capability claim is followed by a specific proof point from
  `proof-points.md`. No claim stands alone.
- **Relevance over impressiveness.** A contextually analogous case beats a bigger
  but distant one.
- **Never stretch.** If there's no directly analogous case, use the closest
  adjacent one and say so honestly.
- **Client names only where cleared.** Otherwise describe by sector ("a global
  networking company", "a leading enterprise security firm").
